You own a niche that enterprise AEC firms desperately need but cannot articulate—institutional knowledge recovery and IP risk mitigation at the firm level. This is not a software feature; it's a liability hedge disguised as productivity software.

2.1 Elevator Pitch Suite

The One-Liner
Sovereign institutional memory for AEC firms—recover design precedent, eliminate re-work, preserve IP without cloud vendor lock-in.
The 30-Second Pitch (The Hook)
"Architects lose institutional knowledge every time a senior designer leaves or a project closes. That costs 10–15% re-work and risk on new bids. We built Nexus Hestia to recover that knowledge automatically—by indexing everything: Revit models, project docs, team decisions. It's like giving your firm a persistent memory. Searches are geometric, not keyword-based—so you find that staircase detail or façade system from 2019 by shape, not by guessing the file name. And it lives on your infrastructure, not someone else's cloud. You own it."
The 60-Second Pitch (Investor/Partner Narrative)
"Architecture firms operate on repeat work: the same façade systems, MEP coordination patterns, structural details appear across projects. But that knowledge is locked in closed Revit files, email chains, and departing team members. The recovery cost is real—Deloitte found AEC managers lose 11.5 hours a week to redundant work.

Nexus Hestia captures that institutional memory automatically. We index Revit models geometrically—you search by shape, not keywords. We layer narrative understanding on top—connecting design moves to the business reasoning behind them. And critically, the system runs on your infrastructure. You own the index. You control the data pipeline.

Right now, we've unified three indexing engines under a single orchestrator. We've signed SK-Etch; we're in active conversations with HOK, Cumming, and GRAFT. The unit economics work: we're at $800–3,000 per seat depending on tier. We're targeting institutional seed in Q4 at $12–15M pre to scale to mid-market AEC.

The market is us and Knowledge Architecture, who are cloud-only. We win on sovereignty and architectural flexibility. That's the wedge."

2.2 Core Message Pillars

Pillar 1: The IP Preservation Imperative
Problem: AEC firms lose 10–15% of project value to institutional amnesia and re-work when knowledge lives in closed files, email, and departing staff.

Position: We recover and index that knowledge automatically, making it searchable and portable across the firm.

Proof: Deloitte Access Economics (2023, n=1,275 firms): AEC managers lose 11.5 hours/week to redundant work. BIM professionals lose 13.4 hours. Image Search alone recovers 30–40% of that time in early pilots.

Messaging: "Eliminate institutional amnesia. Recover design precedent. Reduce re-work."
Pillar 2: Sovereignty as Non-Negotiable
Problem: Cloud-based BIM search tools (Synthesis, cloud Revit) lock firm knowledge into vendor platforms.

Position: We deploy on your infrastructure. You own the index. You control the data pipeline. No vendor lock-in. No regulatory friction.

Proof: Orchestrator and indexers run on Ollama (open-source LLM stack). Dashboard is static HTML + local API. No cloud dependencies. Deployable on-premises or in your private cloud.

Messaging: "Your knowledge. Your infrastructure. Your control."
Pillar 3: Domain-Specific Intelligence Over Generic LLMs
Problem: Generic enterprise LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude enterprise) hallucinate over technical data. They lack geometric reasoning.

Position: We built agents trained on AEC patterns. We reason over geometry, not just text. We understand Revit semantics natively.

Proof: Geometric search finds design moves by spatial similarity. Narrative agents connect design decisions to business context. Confidence triad (0.4 geometric / 0.3 narrative / 0.3 visual) prevents false positives.

Messaging: "AEC-native intelligence. Geometric reasoning. Domain-specific accuracy."
Pillar 4: Architectural Extensibility
Problem: Point-solution BIM tools don't evolve when firms adopt new tools (Twinmotion, Speckle, Grasshopper plugins).

Position: Multi-agent orchestrator means we add new indexers without rebuilding the search engine.

Proof: Unified orchestrator (FastAPI :8090) reconciles Revit Search + Image Search + legacy indexers. Adding a new agent is additive, not a rewrite.

Messaging: "Grows with your tools. No rip-and-replace. Additive innovation."
